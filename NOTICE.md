## What is **public-domain Jain heritage** (used with respect, not owned)

- The **Navkar Mantra** itself — its nine lines, its akshars, its meaning,
  and the pañca-parameṣṭhi (Arihant, Siddha, Acharya, Upadhyay, Sadhu) it
  invokes.
- The traditional **colour associations** of the Panch Parmeshthi (white,
  red, yellow, green, indigo).
- The **Devanagari script** and Sanskrit/Prakrit transliteration
  conventions.
- The broader Jain practice of memorising and reciting the mantra as a
  daily prayer.

Building a learning tool around the mantra does **not** make the mantra
ours. It belongs to the Jain community and is presented here as our shared
inheritance.

## Voiceovers

The "Record your voice" feature (`recorder.html`) lets each user record
their own recitation locally on-device. No third-party voice recordings are
bundled by default.

If, in future releases, a pre-recorded voiceover is bundled with the game,
it will be credited here to its respective reciter or singer — drawn from
public-domain Jain tradition with due credit and respect.
